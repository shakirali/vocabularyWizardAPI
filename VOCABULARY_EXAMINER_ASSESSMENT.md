### ⚠️ AREAS FOR IMPROVEMENT

#### 1. **Meaning Definitions Need Enrichment** (Priority: HIGH)

**Issue:** 506 words (31.8%) have meanings shorter than 25 characters

**Impact:** Short definitions may not provide sufficient context for learners

**Examples of Short Meanings:**
- "extremely small" (miniscule)
- "complete or total" (absolute)
- "sad and disappointed" (crest-fallen)

**Pedagogical Concern:**
- Young learners need **richer context** to grasp nuanced vocabulary
- Definitions should include usage hints or conceptual clarification
- Target length: 35-60 characters for optimal comprehension

**Recommended Enhancement:**
```
❌ Before: "extremely small"
✅ After:  "extremely small; so tiny it is barely visible"

❌ Before: "complete or total"
✅ After:  "complete and without any limits; total and unrestricted"

❌ Before: "sad and disappointed"
✅ After:  "feeling sad and disappointed, especially after a setback"
```

#### 2. **Minor Word Form Mismatches** (Priority: MEDIUM)

**Issue:** 6 words (0.4%) have form variations in examples

**Examples:**
- Word: "category" → Example uses: "categories"
- Word: "dawdle" → Example uses: "dawdling"
- Word: "miniscule" → Example uses: "minuscule" (also spelling variant)

**Pedagogical Concern:**
- Students may not recognize morphological connections
- Particularly challenging for English language learners

**Recommendation:**
- Update examples to include exact word form OR
- Add a note in the example highlighting the connection
- Example: "Stop dawdling (dawdle) or we will miss the bus."

#### 3. **Capitalization Inconsistencies** (Priority: LOW)

**Issue:** 6 sentences start with quotation marks instead of capital letters

**Examples:**
- '"Happy" is a synonym for "joyful".'
- '"As brave as a lion" is a simile.'

**Impact:** Minor formatting issue; does not affect learning

**Recommendation:** Low priority; acceptable as-is for technical/metalinguistic terms
