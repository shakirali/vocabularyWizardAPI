## üî¥ CRITICAL ISSUES IDENTIFIED

### **1. MASSIVE USE OF GENERIC TEMPLATES (87% of sentences)**

**Problem:** 13,839 sentences use generic templates that provide **no contextual clues**.

**Examples of problematic generic sentences:**
- `"The committee decided to _____ the proposal after careful consideration of all factors."`
  - Used for: adhere, adjourn, adopt, adore, adorn, abandon, abate, etc. (306+ words)
  - **Problem:** These words have completely different meanings!

- `"The _____ was clear to everyone present at the meeting."`
  - Used for: 1,102+ different words
  - **Problem:** Works for ANY noun - provides zero clues

- `"The _____ provided crucial context for understanding the broader implications."`
  - Used for: 1,127+ different words
  - **Problem:** Vague, generic, no specific context

**Impact:** 
- Students cannot infer word meanings from context
- Sentences are interchangeable between words
- Fails to meet eleven plus standards for contextual clues

**Problem:** Most sentences do not provide strong contextual clues that help students understand the word's meaning.

**What's Missing:**

- Cause-effect relationships
- Contrast or comparison
- Real-world scenarios

**Example of Poor Sentence:**
