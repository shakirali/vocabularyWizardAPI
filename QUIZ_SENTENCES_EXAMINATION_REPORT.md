## 1. Critical Issues

### 1.1 Vague Sentences (3,048 instances) ⚠️ **CRITICAL**

**Issue:** Sentences lack strong contextual clues and are too vague to effectively test vocabulary understanding.

**Problematic Patterns:**
- "It was _____"
- "The _____ was"
- "It is _____"
- "The situation was _____"
- "It seemed _____"

**Examples:**
```
❌ Level 1 - 'contrast': "The _____ was striking."
❌ Level 1 - 'crucial': "It is _____ to arrive on time."
❌ Level 1 - 'Ablest': "The _____ was clear to everyone present at the meeting."
❌ Level 1 - 'Abode': "The _____ became evident very quickly to all observers."
```

**Impact:** These sentences don't provide enough context for students to determine the correct word. Multiple words could fit, making the quiz ineffective.

**Recommendation:** Replace with sentences that include specific scenarios, actions, or outcomes that clearly indicate the target word.

---

### 1.2 Weak Context (1,071 instances) ⚠️ **HIGH PRIORITY**

**Issue:** Sentences are too short (less than 6 words) and lack sufficient detail.

**Examples:**
```
❌ Level 1 - 'abundant' (5 words): "The garden produced _____ fruit."
❌ Level 1 - 'acquire' (5 words): "Experience helps us _____ wisdom."
❌ Level 1 - 'advance' (4 words): "Science _____s through experimentation."
❌ Level 1 - 'available' (4 words): "Is this seat _____?"
```

**Impact:** Short sentences don't provide enough context clues. Students need more information to make informed choices.

**Recommendation:** Expand sentences to include more context (minimum 8-10 words for level 1-2, 10-12 words for level 3-4).

---

### 1.3 Repetitive Patterns (303 words affected) ⚠️ **HIGH PRIORITY**

**Issue:** Many words have sentences that follow identical or very similar patterns, reducing variety and effectiveness.

**Most Affected Words:**
- 'elusive': 48 sentences, only 5 unique patterns
- 'benevolent': 45 sentences, only 18 unique patterns
- 'cordial': 45 sentences, only 7 unique patterns
- 'disdain': 42 sentences, only 6 unique patterns
- 'abundant': 36 sentences, only 7 unique patterns

**Impact:** Repetitive patterns make quizzes predictable and don't test vocabulary understanding across different contexts.

**Recommendation:** Diversify sentence structures and contexts for each word.

---

### 1.4 Synonym Usage (71 instances) ⚠️ **MEDIUM PRIORITY**

**Issue:** Some sentences contain synonyms of the target word, which gives away the answer.

**Examples:**
```
⚠️ Level 1 - 'gloomy': "The _____ corridor was dark and cold."
   (Contains synonym: 'dark')

⚠️ Level 1 - 'lucid': "The _____ was clear to everyone present at the meeting."
   (Contains synonym: 'clear')

⚠️ Level 1 - 'merry': "Have a _____ Christmas and happy new year!"
   (Contains synonym: 'happy')
```

**Impact:** Students can guess the answer without understanding the target word's meaning.

**Recommendation:** Remove synonyms from sentences or replace with antonyms or neutral words.

---

### 1.5 Multiple Blanks (1 instance) ⚠️ **MINOR**

**Example:**
